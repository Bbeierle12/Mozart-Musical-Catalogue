Biography

Johann Sebastian Bach (1685-1750) was a German composer and musician of the Baroque period. He is known for instrumental compositions such as the Brandenburg Concertos and the Goldberg Variations, and for vocal music such as the St Matthew Passion and the Mass in B minor.

Born into a musical family in Eisenach, Bach received his early musical education from his father and later from his older brother. He held several important positions during his lifetime, including court musician in Weimar, Kapellmeister in Köthen, and Thomaskantor in Leipzig.

Bach's music is revered for its intellectual depth, technical command, artistic beauty, and emotional expressiveness. His works represent the culmination of the Baroque era and have influenced virtually every major composer since.
