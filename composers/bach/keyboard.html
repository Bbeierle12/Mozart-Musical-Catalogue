Bach Keyboard Works

BWV 772-994 • Harpsichord, Clavichord & Early Piano

Overview

Bach's keyboard works represent the pinnacle of Baroque keyboard composition. From the pedagogical Inventions to the monumental Goldberg Variations, these works showcase Bach's mastery of counterpoint and keyboard technique.

The Well-Tempered Clavier

Book I (BWV 846-869)

Composed 1722 • 24 Preludes and Fugues

The first book of The Well-Tempered Clavier demonstrates the possibilities of equal temperament tuning, with a prelude and fugue in each of the 24 major and minor keys.

Book II (BWV 870-893)

Composed 1742 • 24 Preludes and Fugues

Twenty years later, Bach compiled a second set of preludes and fugues, generally more complex and mature than the first book.

Pedagogical Works

Inventions and Sinfonias (BWV 772-801)

30 short pieces for keyboard instruction

  • 15 Two-Part Inventions (BWV 772-786)
  • 15 Three-Part Sinfonias (BWV 787-801)

These works teach independent voice leading and serve as preparation for the Well-Tempered Clavier.

Major Individual Works

Goldberg Variations (BWV 988)

1741 • G major

Aria with 30 variations, one of the most important works in the keyboard repertoire

Italian Concerto (BWV 971)

1735 • F major

Concerto for two-manual harpsichord, imitating the Italian concerto style

Chromatic Fantasia and Fugue (BWV 903)

1720 • D minor

Virtuosic work showcasing Bach's improvisatory style

French Overture (BWV 831)

1735 • B minor

Published in Clavier-Übung II, paired with the Italian Concerto
